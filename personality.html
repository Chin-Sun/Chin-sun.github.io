    Fairy Deer

    Hand-knitted Doll

    I knitted this fairy deer over a month, finding little moments here and there to work on it. In Chinese mythology, this deer is a symbol of good luck, happiness, and long life. Stories say it can bring fortune and wealth, like a magical creature from a world of fairy tales.



    Bamboo Bookmark

    Cloisonné Enamel

    I spent two weeks making this bookmark, which features bamboo - a plant that in Chinese culture stands for the qualities of a true gentleman who maintains his integrity and upright character, no matter the circumstances.

    The design of this bamboo bookmark is crafted using the Cloisonné Enamel technique, an ancient art that outlines areas with metal wires to fill with different colored enamels. This method combines the styles of Chinese painting and Western oil painting to create artwork on a flat surface.



    Rabbit Bookmark

    Cloisonné Enamel

    This bookmark took me two weeks to complete. This Rabbit's moon-viewing bookmark is also made using Cloisonné Enamel's technology.

    The story behind this bookmark is a love story of two enamored rabbits who love each other and watch the moon together on a full moon night.
    You see, the rabbit on the left is secretly looking at the rabbit on the right with full of love. Isn’t it very similar to how we love someone deeply?



    Temari Ball Bracelet

    Handwoven Bracelet

    It took me a week to complete Temari Ball Bracelet. Temari balls, originating from the ancient Chinese game of Cuju, similar to modern-day football, were traditionally made for throwing and play.

    I miniaturized a Temari ball, wove a bracelet, and combined them so it can be worn on the wrist.



    Love Seed Bracelet

    Handwoven Bracelet

    The Love Seed Bracelet took me three days to complete.

    The bracelet is made from the seeds of the red sandalwood tree, often called "love seeds" in Chinese culture, symbolizing love and longing between people.

    Happiness

    Paper Cutting

    Paper cutting is a traditional Chinese art where people cut fancy patterns into paper. It's been around for more than 1,500 years and usually shows things like animals, flowers, or stories with special meanings. People use these paper cutouts to decorate their homes and celebrate special times, hoping to bring good luck and happiness.

    Qilin

    Hand-embroidered Doll

    It took me three weeks to complete the Qilin doll decoration. Qilin refers to the traditional Chinese auspicious animal. The ancients believed that wherever the unicorn appears, there must be auspiciousness. Qilin has a similar meaning to the unicorn in Western culture.

    Guiying Mu T-shirt

    Painting on T-shirt

    I spent three Sundays painting Guiying Mu on this T-shirt. Mu Guiying is a famous hero from Chinese stories, known for her bravery and smart battle plans. She's like Athena from Western stories, showing the power and leadership of a true hero.

    I admire Guiying Mu's incredible achievements, so I wanted her on my T-shirt. It reminds me to be strong and lead bravely, just like her.
